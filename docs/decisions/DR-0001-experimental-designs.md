# DR-0001: Experimental Designs

**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** 2026-03-16  
**Decision makers:** Taren, Bridget, Dana  
**Related issues:** NA

---

## Context

We did not explicitly state if experimental studies would be included or excluded.
In principle, these studies should be included, as they provide the strongest level of evidence.
In practice, manipulated screen time is distinct from ecologically valid screen time, and the majority of studies being harmonised are observational.

The primary issue is if we should be attempting to collect experimental studies where the screen time has been directly manipulated.

---

## Decision

We will include experimental studies where the screen time could reasonably be considered ecologically valid, regardless of the manipulation.

For example, a health intervention that reduces screen time by encouraging physical activity would be included, but a study that manipulates screen time by having participants watch a specific amount of TV in a lab setting would not be included.

---

## Rationale

This decision balances the need for methodological rigour with the goal of harmonising a wide range of datasets.
Researchers directly manipulating screen time in a controlled setting (e.g., lab-based exposure to specific screen content) likely does not reflect real-world screen time behaviours.
Changes in regular screen behaviour due to an intervention (e.g., reducing screen time through a health program) are more likely to be ecologically valid and relevant for understanding the impact of screen time on health outcomes.

---

## Alternatives considered

- We considered removing all experimental designed, but felt this was a violation of the protocol and removed the stronger evidences
- We considered including direct manipulations, but felt this did not provide useful generalisable insights

Staff who are screening have been advised on how to proceed.
